# Crosses
* Crosses, the character ✠, are used in the text to indicate when it is customary for people to make the sign of the cross over themselves. 
* So far, only one of them has made it into the bulletin, in the Absolution following the Confession of Sin. There it is in the right place, but improperly styled. It should not be italic. 
* There should also be crosses
** At the end of the Celebrant’s portion of the Opening Acclamation, i.e. Bless the Lord who forgives all our sins. ✠
** At the end of the Nicene Creed, We look for the resurrection of the dead ✠, and the life of the world to come.
** In the Prayers of the People, whenever the dead are mentioned, for example:
*** (Prayer IV) We commend to your mercy all who have died ✠, that your will for them
*** (Prayer III) Give to the departed eternal rest ✠; 
** In the Eucharistic Prayer:
*** In the Sanctus: Blessed is he who comes in the name of the Lord.
*** After the words of Institution: 
**** “Take, eat: This is my Body, which is given for you. Do this for the remembrance of me." ✠
**** Whenever you drink it, do this for the remembrance of me." ✠
** In any blessing at the end of the service after the name of the Trinity (not an issue during Lent, but in all other seasons)
